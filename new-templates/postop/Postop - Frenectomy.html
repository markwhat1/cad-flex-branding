Frenectomy Surgery
Post-Operative Instructions

Today you had a frenectomy with the Periolase MVP-7 dental laser. These instructions will help your wound heal cleanly and stay open long enough for the new attachment to form correctly. You also have a separate Stretching Exercises sheet. Those exercises are the most important part of your recovery. Begin them the day after surgery, 24 hours after your appointment.

What's Normal in the First 48 Hours

  • Mild swelling of the lip or surrounding gum.
  • Soreness or tenderness, especially when smiling or talking.
  • Slight oozing or pinkish saliva for a few hours.
  • A mild stinging sensation when eating salty, acidic, or hot foods.
You will see a yellow or white film over the surgical site. This is not infection and not a sign that something is wrong. The Periolase laser seals tissue as it cuts, and this protective layer (called a pseudomembrane) covers the wound for 5 to 10 days while new tissue forms underneath. Do not try to brush, scrape, or peel it off. It will slough away on its own.

Pain Management

For most frenectomy patients, over-the-counter medication is enough. Take the following at the same time every 4 to 6 hours (no more than 4 times in any 24-hour period):

  • 3 (three) 200 mg ibuprofen tablets (e.g., Motrin, Advil). Maximum in 24 hours: 3200 mg.
  • 2 (two) 325 mg acetaminophen tablets (Tylenol). Maximum in 24 hours: 3000 mg.

Continue this for the first 48 to 72 hours, even if you have no discomfort. Taking it on a schedule keeps inflammation under control and makes the stretching exercises easier to perform.

If you have a medical condition or take a medication that prevents you from using ibuprofen or acetaminophen, please call our office for guidance.

Diet

  • First 24 hours: Soft, room-temperature foods. Yogurt, eggs, mashed potato, applesauce, oatmeal, smoothies (eaten with a spoon, not a straw).
  • Days 2 through 5: Avoid spicy, acidic, crunchy, and very hot foods. They will sting and may dislodge the pseudomembrane.
  • No straws for the first 24 hours. Suction can lift the pseudomembrane.
  • No alcohol for at least 48 hours.

Oral Hygiene

  • Brush and floss the rest of your mouth normally starting tonight.
  • Avoid the surgical site directly for the first 24 hours.
  • Beginning tomorrow, rinse gently with warm salt water (1 teaspoon of salt in 8 oz of warm water) 4 to 6 times per day, especially after meals.
  • Do not use chlorhexidine (Peridex) on this site unless we have specifically prescribed it. Chlorhexidine can interfere with the protective pseudomembrane on a laser wound.

Activity

  • Rest for the remainder of today.
  • Avoid strenuous exercise for 48 hours. Increased blood pressure can cause new bleeding.
  • Do not smoke or vape. Smoking dramatically slows wound healing and increases the risk of frenum reattachment.

Stretching Exercises

This is the single most important thing you can do to prevent your frenum from reattaching. You have a separate Stretching Exercises sheet. Begin the day after surgery, 24 hours after your appointment, and continue through your 3-week follow-up visit. If you have lost the sheet, please call our office and we will send another copy.

Follow-Up

We will see you back 3 weeks after your procedure for a healing check.

Please Call Us If

  • Bleeding does not stop after 30 minutes of firm pressure.
  • Swelling continues to worsen after the third day.
  • You develop a fever above 101°F.
  • Pain is severe and not relieved by ibuprofen and acetaminophen taken on schedule.
  • You see signs of infection: pus, foul taste, or spreading redness.
