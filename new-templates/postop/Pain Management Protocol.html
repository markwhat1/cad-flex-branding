Pain Management Protocol
Post-Procedure Instructions

The management of your pain following oral surgery, root canal, or periodontal treatment is of the utmost importance. Extensive research and published studies involving over 44,000 patients have shown that this non-narcotic pain management protocol is as effective as, has fewer side effects than, and is less expensive than traditional narcotic medications (Vicodin, Percocet, Norco, Tylenol with Codeine, etc.).

Important: This protocol's dosage and frequency must be followed exactly in order to be effective. The maximum 24-hour doses for ibuprofen and acetaminophen should never be exceeded.
• Ibuprofen maximum in 24 hours: 3200 mg
• Acetaminophen maximum in 24 hours: 3000 mg

Instructions

Take the following at the same time every 4–6 hours (no more than 4 times in any 24-hour period):

  • 3 (three) 200 mg ibuprofen tablets (over-the-counter strength, e.g. Motrin, Advil)
  • 2 (two) 325 mg acetaminophen (Tylenol)

Continue this for the first 72 hours even if you are having no discomfort. Taking it by the clock for the first 72 hours is key to this drug regimen working properly, relieving your pain, and aiding the healing process.
