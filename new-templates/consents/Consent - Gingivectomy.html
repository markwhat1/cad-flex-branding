Gingivectomy Surgery
Informed Consent

Please read this document and provide the appropriate signature. Ask us to clarify anything you do not understand.

Purpose

I have been informed that the purpose of this procedure is to remove overgrown gum tissue covering the dental implants so that Dr. Whatcott can place a healing abutment and ultimately restore the dental implants.

Alternatives to the Suggested Treatment

These may include no treatment, with the expectation of continued tissue overgrowth and inability to restore the dental implants.

Risks Related to the Procedure

Risks which may be related to periodontal surgery include, but are not limited to: bleeding, swelling, mild to moderate pain, transient but on occasion permanent numbness of the lip, tongue, chin, or gum, jaw joint injuries or associated muscle spasm, and tooth sensitivity to hot, cold, sweets, or acidic foods. Shrinkage of the gum may occur. Risks related to the anesthetics might include, but are not limited to: bruising, numbness, pain, soreness, or discoloration at the site of anesthetic injections.

No Warranty or Guarantee

I hereby acknowledge that no guarantee, warranty, or assurance has been given to me that the proposed therapy will be completely successful in eradicating my condition. It is anticipated that the procedure will provide benefit in reducing the excess gingiva and allow Dr. Whatcott to place a healing abutment.

Compliance with Self-Care Instructions

I understand that excessive smoking and/or alcohol intake may affect gum healing and may limit the successful outcome of the procedure. I agree to follow instructions related to my own daily care of my mouth. I agree to report for my appointments following surgery as suggested so that my healing may be monitored and so that Dr. Whatcott can evaluate and report on the outcome upon completion of healing.
