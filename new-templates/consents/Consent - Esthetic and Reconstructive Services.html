Informed Consent

Risks and Complications

I understand that as with any dental procedure, there are possible complications of which I must be aware. These include, but are not limited to: limited oral function; post-operative pain, bleeding; infection or abscess which may require treatment or drainage; temporary bruising of the face; allergic reactions to metals and medications; a change in sensation or numbness to the lip, chin, face, and/or tongue which may be temporary or permanent in nature; periodontal infection or condition requiring additional treatment; injury to the teeth; temporomandibular joint (jaw) problems requiring additional treatment; and poor healing which may result in an alteration or change in the planned treatment.

Although unusual, an unexpected complication or less-than-desired result can occur and may result in the need for further surgery, possible hospitalization, tests, prolonged recovery, loss of work time, and the possibility of further expense.

Crowns, Bridges, and Veneers

I understand that basic crown, bridge, and veneer preparations may result in the considerable reduction of tooth structure when properly done. Tissue and temperature sensitivity could be experienced for an extended healing period. In some instances, root canal therapy may be required to eliminate the sensitive tissues inside the tooth.

I understand that it may be necessary to remove sensitive tooth structure in order to place veneers. The dental lab and Dr. Whatcott will do their very best to create a shade that is as close as possible to the shade I chose, but the shade may not be an exact match.

Effort will be made to make the teeth appear as straight as possible with veneers, but because of the existing position of my natural teeth, this cannot be guaranteed. Veneers usually require very little tooth modification, but some teeth, because of their present position and my bite, may require more modification than others. Veneers will add minimal bulk to my teeth, and I will need to get used to this addition.

Home Care

I understand that excellent home care techniques, using a variety of aids (i.e., bite guards), will add considerably to the successful outcome of my restorative program. It will be important for me to follow the home care instructions, both written and oral, very carefully.

No Warranty or Guarantee

Although favorable results are expected, no guarantee or warranty of expectations, refunds of any kind, either expressed or implied, has been made. This is due to human variables associated with individual healing and responses to surgery and recovery. The attendant risks of surgery and anesthesia have been explained to me.
