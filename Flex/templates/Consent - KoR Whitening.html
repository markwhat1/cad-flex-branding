Purpose

Whitening lightens the natural color of your teeth using professional-strength gel. The goal is a lighter, brighter smile while maintaining healthy enamel and gums.

Treatment Methods

  1. In-Office Whitening – Performed in a single visit. Results appear quickly but may fade sooner than other methods.
  2. At-Home Whitening – Custom trays worn for several weeks. Slower change but more stable, longer-lasting results.
  3. Combination Whitening – Both in-office and at-home steps for the most even and predictable outcome.

Benefits

  • Improved tooth color and smile appearance
  • Non-invasive and enamel-safe when used correctly

Risks and Limitations

  • Temporary tooth or gum sensitivity (usually 24–48 hours)
  • Uneven whitening in areas with spots or stains
  • Existing fillings, crowns, or veneers will not lighten
  • Resistant discoloration (tetracycline, fluorosis) may require additional sessions or fees
  • Not recommended during pregnancy; nursing patients should consult their physician

Expectations

Results vary by individual. A slight shade difference between gumline and tooth edge is normal. Some dark or white areas may remain visible even after full treatment.

Maintenance

Long-term whiteness depends on following your maintenance plan. Brushing with a non-abrasive toothpaste and occasional touch-ups help preserve results. Moderate coffee, tea, and wine are acceptable if followed by rinsing or water.

Emergency Instructions

If you experience unusual pain, swelling, or allergic reaction, stop using your trays and contact this office immediately.
