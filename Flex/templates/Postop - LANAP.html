LANAP Periodontal Therapy
Post-Operative Instructions

What's Normal After Treatment

Your gums may change color after laser therapy. Gray, yellow, red, blue, purple, and "stringy" tissue are all normal responses and nothing to worry about. You may also notice:

  • Light bleeding or oozing (this looks worse than it is when mixed with saliva)
  • Mild swelling, soreness, or tooth sensitivity. Swelling may increase over the first 2 to 3 days before improving.
  • A medicinal taste from Peridex rinse. Peridex can also cause temporary staining on your teeth and tongue with prolonged use; this is harmless and can be polished off at your next visit.

If you notice bleeding, apply gentle pressure to the area. If you can't pinpoint the source, rinse gently with ice water and hold a damp tea bag against the general area.

What's Not Normal

Most patients recover smoothly, but contact us right away if you experience any of the following:

Call our office at (303) 986-9337 if you notice:

  • Prolonged or severe pain that isn't managed by ibuprofen
  • Prolonged or excessive bleeding
  • Fever or persistently elevated temperature
  • Blisters or sores on the roof of your mouth

Protect Your Healing

For at least one week after treatment, please avoid all of the following:

  • Smoking, vaping, or any tobacco or marijuana products. These slow healing and can compromise your results.
  • Alcohol for at least one week. Alcohol impairs your body's ability to heal tissue and increases the risk of bleeding when combined with ibuprofen.
  • Strenuous physical activity such as heavy lifting, running, and intense exercise. An elevated heart rate increases swelling, bleeding, and discomfort.

Keeping Your Results

LANAP gives your gums a fresh start, but the long-term success of this treatment depends on what happens next. The habits that led to gum disease can lead to it again if they don't change. Here's how to protect the investment you've made in your health:

  • Commit to better daily oral hygiene. Thorough brushing, flossing, and daily Waterpik use is the single most important thing you can do. We'll help you build these habits at each visit.
  • Keep every scheduled appointment. Your follow-up visits and periodontal maintenance cleanings are not optional. They allow us to monitor your healing, catch problems early, and clean areas you can't reach at home. Missing appointments puts your results at risk.
  • Be patient with the process. Your gums, bone, and ligaments may continue improving for up to 18 months after treatment. Consistent care during this time makes a real difference in your outcome.
Having treatment over two days? Follow the "Day of Treatment" instructions after each session. All other timelines (Days 1–3, Days 4–7, etc.) begin after your last treatment session.
Day of Treatment Today

Comfort

  • Apply an ice pack to the outside of your face, 20 minutes per hour, until bedtime. Do not use ice beyond today.
  • Take your prescribed ibuprofen as directed. This helps with both swelling and pain. Ibuprofen is non-narcotic and won't affect your ability to drive.
  • Take it easy. Rest for the remainder of the day and avoid physical activity.

Eating

  • Liquids only today. See the diet guide at the end of this page for ideas.
  • Do not use a straw. The suction can disturb the blood clots that are forming to protect your gums.
  • Avoid spicy and very hot foods or drinks.

Oral Care

  • Do not rinse your mouth today.
  • Do not floss or use a Waterpik on the treated area.
  • You may gently brush the chewing surfaces of your teeth. Keep bristles away from the gumline on treated areas.
  • You may brush and floss normally in areas that were not treated. If your entire mouth was treated, limit brushing to chewing surfaces only.
  • Avoid pressing your tongue or cheek against the treated area.
If your medications were changed for this procedure: If you were taken off blood thinners (such as Coumadin or Warfarin) or had any other medications adjusted before your treatment, contact your physician as soon as possible to find out when to resume them. This is important and should not be delayed. Continue taking all other medications, vitamins, and supplements as prescribed by your doctors.
Days 1 – 3 Liquid diet phase

Eating

  • Continue a liquid-only diet. Your goal is to protect the tiny clots visible along your gumline. Think of them as a band-aid between your gums and teeth.
  • Still no straws. Eat with a spoon if needed.
  • Keep up your nutrition. Protein shakes, blended soups, and smoothies (no seeds) are all great options.

Rinsing

  • Starting the day after your last treatment session, gently rinse with Peridex three times a day. Do not swish aggressively or spit forcefully. Instead, let the rinse slosh gently from side to side, then open your mouth and let it fall into the sink.
  • Between Peridex rinses, gently rinse with warm salt water three times a day (½ teaspoon of salt in 8 oz. of warm water).
  • As the days progress, you can begin rinsing with a bit more vigor.

Medications

  • Continue taking ibuprofen for the full three days, then as needed for any discomfort.
  • If you were prescribed antibiotics, take the entire course as directed, even if you feel fine. Call us if you experience nausea and we can provide help with that.
  • Continue taking any prescribed medications, vitamins, or supplements.
Days 4 – 7 Mushy diet phase

Eating

  • You can move to soft, mushy foods like scrambled eggs, mashed potatoes, yogurt, and well-cooked pasta. See the diet guidance at the end of this page for more ideas.
  • If only one side of your mouth was treated, chew on the opposite side. If both sides were treated, chew carefully and stick with the softest foods on the list.

Oral Care

  • Gently brush the chewing surfaces of your teeth. Keep bristles away from the gumline on treated areas.
  • Continue your Peridex and salt water rinses.
  • Do not floss or use a Waterpik on the treated area.
Day 8 through 1 Month Continued healing

Eating

  • After 7 to 10 days, you will notice the white material along your gumline starting to disappear. Once it's gone, you can begin introducing soft foods like pasta, fish, chicken, and steamed vegetables.
  • Continue making smart food choices through the first month. Softer foods are better while healing continues beneath the surface. If your gums get sore or irritated with a certain food, move back to softer options until healing has progressed further.

Oral Care

  • Starting around one week, you can begin brushing near the treated area with your post-surgical toothbrush. Place your bristles on the gums below the tooth and gently roll toward the tooth surface. Do not push bristles into the gumline. You may use toothpaste.
  • Do not use an electric toothbrush on the treated area until your one-month appointment. We will let you know when it's safe to switch back.
  • Flossing the treated area typically begins around one month after treatment. We'll let you know when you're ready. When you do start, slide the floss gently between your teeth only and avoid pushing it under the gums until we give you the go-ahead.
  • Do not use a Waterpik until we clear you. This usually happens around three months after treatment.
Soreness is a sign of healing. Beginning around 2 weeks after treatment (and sometimes lasting several months), your teeth may feel sore or tender as bone and ligaments regenerate. This is a good sign, but it can also mean your bite needs further adjustment. Let us know and we'll take care of it.

You may also notice small spaces between your teeth as swelling goes down and diseased tissue is removed. These spaces often fill in over time, especially once your bite is properly balanced.

Most surface healing takes 2 to 4 weeks. Deeper areas take several months to fully heal.

Avoid These Foods for the First Month

While your gums are healing, stay away from anything that could irritate the tissue or get stuck between your teeth:

  • Hard, crunchy, or raw foods (chips, nuts, hard candy, croutons, raw vegetables, salads)
  • Chewy or sticky foods (gum, caramel, jerky)
  • Foods with small seeds or hard pieces (seeded berries, popcorn)
  • Meat that shreds and can lodge under the gumline

Diet Guide by Phase

Take your daily vitamins throughout recovery. Remember: no straws during the liquid phase.

Liquid DietDays 1 – 3 after treatment
  • Bone broth, chicken broth, or vegetable broth
  • Creamed soups blended until smooth
  • Protein shakes or meal replacement drinks (Ensure, Premier Protein, etc.)
  • Smoothies with soft fruit, no seeds
  • Drinkable yogurt or kefir
  • Milkshakes (eat with a spoon, no straw)
  • Juice without pulp (avoid citrus/acidic juices)
  • Warm tea or coffee (not hot)
  • Pudding, custard, or mousse
  • Melted ice cream or frozen yogurt
  • Blended oatmeal thinned with milk
  • Miso soup (strained)
Mushy DietDays 4 – 7 after treatment
  • Scrambled eggs, omelets, or soft-boiled eggs
  • Mashed potatoes, sweet potatoes, or butternut squash
  • Mashed banana, mashed avocado, or applesauce
  • Cottage cheese, ricotta, or soft cheese
  • Yogurt, pudding, Jell-O, or ice cream
  • Cream of wheat, grits, or soft oatmeal
  • Mashed steamed vegetables (carrots, peas, squash)
  • Creamed soups with soft pieces
  • Hummus or refried beans
  • Ripe melon or soft canned fruit, no seeds
  • Macaroni and cheese or well-cooked soft pasta
  • Mashed or pureed fruit, no seeds
