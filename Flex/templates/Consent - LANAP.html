LANAP Periodontal Therapy
Informed Consent

Please read this document carefully. It explains the LANAP laser procedure, what to expect, the risks involved, and your role in a successful outcome. Ask Dr. Whatcott if anything is unclear.

Your Diagnosis

  1. I have had a consultation with Dr. Whatcott, who has examined my mouth and diagnosed me with periodontal (gum) disease. Dr. Whatcott has recommended LANAP (Laser Assisted New Attachment Procedure) as a treatment for my condition.
  2. Dr. Whatcott has explained alternative treatment options, including traditional periodontal surgery (such as flap surgery and other conventional methods). I have considered these alternatives and have chosen to proceed with LANAP treatment.

About the Procedure

  1. I understand that LANAP treatment involves one or more surgical sessions using a specialized dental laser. Local anesthesia will be used, and additional sedation or pain management may be provided at Dr. Whatcott's professional judgment.
  2. I authorize Dr. Whatcott and the LANAP treatment team to perform LANAP treatment and any other procedures deemed necessary during the planned course of care. If a modification to the treatment plan is needed based on clinical findings, I authorize Dr. Whatcott to make that adjustment in my best interest.

Expected Outcomes

  1. Based on clinical studies, approximately 87% of LANAP cases have not required retreatment for more than five years, barring unforeseen health or accident-related complications.
  2. I understand that results depend on each person's individual healing response. There is no way to guarantee a specific outcome for any patient.

Risks and Possible Complications

  1. Possible effects of LANAP treatment include pain, swelling, infection, bruising or discoloration, temporary numbness, spaces between teeth, tissue shrinkage, gum recession, and exposure of root surfaces. The duration and severity of these effects varies from person to person. After healing, some areas may need additional spot treatment or bite adjustment.
  2. "Occlusal adjustment" (bite adjustment) has been fully explained to me. I understand that adjusting my bite is an essential part of LANAP treatment and continues at regular examination appointments. I understand that:
    • My full cooperation is required for this process to succeed.
    • Until my bite is fully adjusted, I may experience temporary jaw pain, muscle soreness, headaches, tooth sensitivity, or cheek biting.
    • Adjusting crowns may remove porcelain, expose underlying material, or require crown replacement.
    • Failure to complete all phases of bite adjustment may result in ongoing pain or jaw problems.

Severe Periodontal Disease

  1. I understand that severe gum disease (Case Type III or IV) with pocket measurements of 10mm or more may require a second round of LANAP treatment at the same fee as the initial treatment. This retreatment may be needed on individual teeth or throughout the entire mouth, depending on the extent of active disease.

What Happens Without Treatment

  1. Dr. Whatcott has explained that if I choose not to undergo treatment, my gum and bone disease may continue to progress. This can lead to:
    • Loss of teeth due to bone loss or bite-related damage.
    • Gum infections, including acute infections such as ANUG.
    • Continued tartar buildup contributing to further bone loss.
    • The eventual need for removable partial dentures if teeth are lost and not replaced.
    • Increased stress on remaining teeth if missing teeth are not replaced, potentially accelerating further bone loss and gum disease.

Your Responsibilities

  1. Dr. Whatcott has explained the importance of proper oral hygiene following LANAP treatment. I agree to follow the home care instructions and dietary recommendations provided to me.
  2. I understand that I must attend follow-up and hygiene appointments at least once every three months, or more frequently if Dr. Whatcott requires. These visits are essential for monitoring my healing and the long-term success of treatment.
  3. I understand that smoking, vaping, tobacco use, and heavy alcohol consumption can impair gum tissue healing and reduce the longevity of LANAP treatment. I also understand that calcium balance and hormonal factors can affect ongoing bone health.
  4. I understand that completing all phases of recommended treatment is necessary for the best possible outcome, and I agree to do so.

Health History

  1. I confirm that I have provided an accurate and complete health history. To the best of my knowledge, I have not withheld any information about my medical or mental health, including any allergies or unusual reactions to drugs, foods, insect bites, anesthetics, or other materials.

Photo and Media Authorization

  1. If Dr. Whatcott considers it appropriate, I authorize photos, videos, or x-rays to be taken of my mouth during treatment. I understand these materials may be used for educational purposes to help train other dental professionals in the LANAP protocol.
By signing below, I confirm that I have read and understand this entire document, that all of my questions have been answered, and that I consent to proceed with LANAP treatment as described above.
