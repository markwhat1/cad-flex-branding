Purpose and Background

The Buteyko mouth taping technique is used to evaluate your ability to breathe nasally during sleep and to determine if consistent nasal breathing improves your specific dental signs and symptoms.

This protocol is based on the clinical guidelines of the Buteyko Breathing Clinic, as outlined by Patrick McKeown in the book Close Your Mouth. Nasal breathing techniques have been studied for their benefits in respiratory health, and similar methods (such as chin straps) have long been used by sleep physicians to supplement CPAP therapy.

Safety and Preparation

Before beginning this therapy, please review the following safety guidelines:

  • Nasal Obstructions: If you have any physical obstructions that prevent nasal breathing, or if you are scheduled for nasal or sinus surgery, please inform our staff immediately.
  • Acclimation: Do not use the tape while asleep until you have practiced the technique while awake. You must feel completely comfortable breathing only through your nose before proceeding to nighttime use.
  • CPAP Users: If you use a CPAP unit, you must discuss this with our team before attempting mouth taping, as the tape can interfere with the performance of certain mask types.

Potential Risks and Considerations

While generally safe, nasal breathing therapy may involve certain challenges, including but not limited to:

  • Feelings of anxiety or claustrophobia.
  • Initial difficulty or discomfort breathing solely through the nose.
  • Skin irritation from the adhesive tape.
  • Potential interference with other sleep equipment (CPAP).
