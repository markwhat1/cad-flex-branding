Extractions
Post-Operative Instructions
  • If you received an injection, please try to avoid eating on that side until the anesthetic wears off to prevent injury to your mouth. The injection site may feel sore for several days. Tylenol or Advil may help with discomfort.
  • Mild swelling, bruising, and slight bleeding after an extraction is normal.
  • If you were given gauze, make sure to keep pressure on the extraction site to stop bleeding. Moisten the gauze before use. Please keep biting on the gauze for at least 1 hour. If severe bleeding persists after 5 hours, please call the office immediately.
  • Please avoid spitting, drinking through a straw, smoking, or vigorous rinsing for at least 72 hours. Creating any kind of suction in your mouth may disrupt the blood clot in the extraction site, causing a DRY SOCKET, a very painful condition which is difficult to treat and may cause intense pain for up to 1 week.
  • It is common for the extraction site to feel sore to touch for at least 2 weeks and longer in some cases. Depending on the difficulty of the extraction and your body's reparative ability, complete healing may take longer. It is normal for the extraction site to not fill in completely for at least several weeks.
  • If we prescribed antibiotics, please take the full course EVEN IF THE AREA FEELS NORMAL.
  • AFTER THE FIRST 24 HOURS, YOU CAN RINSE WITH WARM SALT WATER especially after meals to help reduce swelling even further and prevent food particles from contaminating the site. Use 1 teaspoon of salt per 1 cup of water and gently rinse.
  • Take painkillers as prescribed, if applicable. Otherwise, take Tylenol (acetaminophen) and Advil (ibuprofen) according to the following instructions.

Pain Management Instructions

Take the following at the same time every 4-6 hours (no more than 4 times in any 24-hour period):

  • 3 (three) 200 mg ibuprofen tablets (over-the-counter strength, ex. Motrin, Advil)
    • Ibuprofen maximum in 24 hours is 3200 mg.
  • 2 (two) 325 mg acetaminophen (Tylenol)
    • Acetaminophen maximum in 24 hours is 3000 mg.

Continue this for the first 72 hours even if you are having no discomfort. Taking it by the clock for the first 72 hours is key to this drug regimen working properly, relieving your pain, and aiding the healing process.
